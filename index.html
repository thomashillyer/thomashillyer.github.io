EUS Board of Governors

As one of the members of the very first board, my role has been to work with the others to define the role the board plays in the EUS.

Past Roles

EUS Webmaster

2016 - 2018

The webmaster for the Engineering Undergraduate Society of McGill is responsible for creating, updating, and managing over 50 sites on the mcgilleus.ca domain.

I have implemented HTTPS everywhere, created the Frosh 2016 and Frosh 2017 websites, and improved the SEO of all of our sites. Moving forward, my goal is to strengthen the online brand of the EUS by creating website templates for all departments, clubs, committees, and design teams, and students' personal sites.

We run our own Apache server on campus to host all our sites. Managing all the security aspects ourselves leads to a lot of learning experiences. In early 2017, our server was infected with a virus that replaced a lot of our websites with spam links. This was quite devastating as we didn't have any current backups, but it showed us our weaknesses. Since then we have improved security on the server, cleaned up the implementation and old files, and added many new sites that provide great services to our fellow students in engineering. More information about this role can be found on the wiki.
ECSESS VP Internal

2018

The Vice-President of Internal Affairs of the Electrical, Computer, Software Engineering Student Society is responsible for planning social events, holding office hours, and attending the ECSESS council.

As the chief spirit officer I view it as my role to encourage these students to lead a healthier work-life balance and get to know their fellow classmates a little better. My aim in this role is to create and organize events that promote camaraderie and participation within our department.
CodeJam: DataDive Hackathon Chair

2017

Throughout 2017 I was the chair of the organizing committee for a data analytics hackathon for McGill students. The event took place in mid-November and was a smashing success. We were lucky enough to work alongside great companies like Amazon Web Services, Morgan Stanley, and Microsoft and have teams from each join us for the event.

My role as chair included managing and delegating tasks to the committee, coordinating sponsorship, and designing the website. We had over 200 participants and gave out over $3000 in prizes to our winners.

There were multiple data categories and students said that they loved the freedom they had in this event to create whatever they wanted.
